Privacy Policy

Overview

Flit is a geography-based flight game. We take your privacy seriously. This policy describes what data we collect, how it is stored, how long we keep it, and what rights you have over it.

Short version: We collect the minimum data needed to make the game work — your account details, gameplay stats, and anonymous crash reports. We do not sell your data, and we do not currently run advertising or behavioural-analytics tracking. We do rely on a small number of third-party infrastructure providers (described below) to run the game, and ads or in-app purchases may be introduced in a future update. You can delete your account and its associated data at any time from within the app.

Data We Collect

We collect only what is necessary to operate the game and improve reliability.

Data Why Stored
Email address Account creation, login, and account recovery Supabase (encrypted)
Username Your visible pilot name in the game Supabase
Game statistics Scores, round times, games played, accuracy — so progress is saved across devices Supabase
Avatar style parameters The hair, eyes, colours, and other style choices you pick for your pilot avatar Not stored by us — sent to DiceBear each time an avatar is rendered (see Third-Party Services below)
Error telemetry Crash reports to help us fix bugs — contains a random session UUID, app version, platform (web/iOS/Android), and the error message and stack trace Vercel (ephemeral)

What error telemetry does NOT include:

Error logs never contain your email address, user ID, username, device identifier, IP address, or browser fingerprint. The platform field is a coarse label (web-browser, ios-device, android-device) — not a User-Agent string.

The session UUID included in each report is randomly generated on your device for that session only — it is not linked to your account and cannot be used to identify you. It exists purely to group related error events together for debugging.

URL query parameters are stripped before logging — only the page path is recorded, never any tokens, session IDs, or other values that may appear in query strings. Browser user-agent strings are not collected.

How Your Data Is Stored

Account data (email, username, game statistics) is stored in Supabase, a cloud PostgreSQL database hosted on AWS infrastructure. Connections are TLS-encrypted. Passwords are hashed using industry-standard bcrypt — we never see your plaintext password.

Error telemetry is received by a Vercel serverless function and held in an ephemeral in-memory buffer. Errors are periodically flushed to a private GitHub repository log file for triage, then discarded. Vercel functions are stateless — the buffer is cleared on every cold start. Error logs are not linked to any user account.

Avatar images are generated by DiceBear, a third-party open-source avatar service. When you create or edit your pilot avatar, the style parameters you chose (hair, eyes, colours, accessories, etc.) are sent to DiceBear's API so it can render the image. We do not send your email, username, or account ID to DiceBear — only the avatar style parameters. Rendered avatars are not stored by us; they are requested fresh from DiceBear as needed.

Map tiles shown during gameplay are fetched directly from OpenStreetMap and, depending on the map style you select in Settings, other open map-tile providers (CARTO, OpenTopoMap). These are standard map-tile image requests containing only the map coordinates needed to display the relevant tile — no account information is sent.

We do not operate our own database or tile servers. All infrastructure is managed by reputable third-party providers (Supabase, Vercel, GitHub, DiceBear, OpenStreetMap and other open map-tile providers) under their own security programs and privacy policies.

Data Retention

Data type Retention
Account data (email, username, stats) Kept until you delete your account. Deleted within 30 days of account deletion request.
Error telemetry Ephemeral. Purged from memory on every server restart. GitHub log entries are pruned regularly.
Local storage (web) Auth session token only. Cleared when you sign out.
Avatar & map tile requests Not retained by us. These are passthrough requests made directly from your device to DiceBear and OpenStreetMap-style providers; see their respective privacy policies for how they handle request data (e.g. IP address in their own server logs).

Your Rights

You have the following rights over your data, exercisable directly from within the app:

  • Right to access — You can view all your game statistics and profile data from the Profile screen at any time.
  • Right to export — You can export all your data as a JSON file from the Profile screen. The export includes your username, email, and full game history.
  • Right to deletion — You can delete your account from the Profile screen. This permanently removes your email, username, and all associated game data from Supabase. Deletion is irreversible.
  • Right to rectification — You can update your username and display name from within the app.

To exercise any right not available in-app, or to request deletion of historical error log entries, contact us at jamiebright1@gmail.com.

Third-Party Services & Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information, and we do not share it with data brokers. To build and run Flit, however, we do rely on a small number of third-party service providers, each of which processes a limited slice of data on our behalf:

Provider Purpose Data involved
Supabase Authentication & database Email, username, gameplay stats/scores
Vercel App hosting & crash/error telemetry Session UUID, app version, platform, error message/stack trace — never email or user ID
DiceBear Avatar image generation Avatar style parameters only — no account information
OpenStreetMap (and other open tile providers) Map tile imagery during gameplay Map tile coordinates only — no account information

These providers act as data processors under their own privacy policies and terms of service, and do not use the data we send them for their own advertising or profiling purposes. We do not currently share data with any other third parties.

Analytics, Tracking & Future Advertising

Flit does not currently use Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any other third-party behavioural-analytics SDK, and there are no advertising or tracking pixels in the app today. The only telemetry we collect is the anonymous crash/error reporting described above.

This may change in the future. We may introduce advertising and/or in-app purchases in a future update (see our Terms of Service). If we do, we will update this policy before those features ship, and any advertising or analytics SDK we add will be disclosed here along with what data it collects.

Cookies & Local Storage

The Flit web app does not use cookies. On web, we use localStorage solely to persist your Supabase auth session token between page loads, so you do not have to log in every time you open the app. No other data is written to local storage.

Native iOS and Android apps use the platform's secure keychain/keystore for session persistence — no cookies are involved.

Children & COPPA

Flit is a geography education game suitable for all ages. However, creating an account requires an email address. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 years of age without verifiable parental consent, in compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child under 13 has created an account without your consent, please contact us at jamiebright1@gmail.com and we will delete the account promptly.
